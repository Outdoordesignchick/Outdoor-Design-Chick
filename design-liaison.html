What Design Liaison Means

The Person Who Keeps
Your Project Honest

Design liaison is a simple idea with a big impact: having someone who deeply understands your design stay engaged through the construction process. Not as a project manager, not as a contractor — as your design advocate.

Meagen built her career on construction sites before she ever opened a design studio. She knows how contractors think, where interpretations get made, and what questions need to be answered in the field before they become expensive problems.

"I've seen too many beautiful designs get lost in translation between the drawing and the dig. I stay in to make sure that doesn't happen to you."

Whether you've worked with Meagen from the beginning or you're coming to her mid-project, design liaison keeps your vision intact all the way to the finished space.

What's Included

Design Liaison
in Practice

From the first contractor walkthrough to the final punch list, Meagen is reachable, responsive, and ready to protect the integrity of your design at every stage.

01

Pre-Construction Walkthrough

Meagen walks the site with your contractor before work begins — reviewing the plans together, clarifying design intent, and flagging anything that needs to be resolved before the first shovel goes in.

02

Contractor Communication

Questions that come up mid-build don't have to wait. Meagen is the direct line between your contractor's field questions and a design-informed answer — so work doesn't stall and improvisation doesn't happen.

03

Site Visits When Needed

For key construction milestones — pool shell review, hardscape layout confirmation, structure framing — Meagen visits in person to verify the build is tracking true to the design before it's too late to adjust.

04

Material & Substitution Review

When a contractor proposes a material substitution — because something is unavailable, backordered, or over budget — Meagen evaluates the alternative against the design intent before it gets approved or rejected.

05

Client Decision Support

Mid-build decisions feel different when you're standing in a half-finished backyard. Meagen helps clients make confident, design-aligned choices in the moment — without the costly second-guessing that derails timelines.

06

Final Walkthrough & Sign-Off

At project completion, Meagen walks the finished space against the original design — confirming materials, finishes, and details are all where they should be before you accept the build.

Why It Matters

Three Problems
Design Liaison Prevents

Every one of these happens on outdoor builds without a design advocate. None of them have to happen on yours.

Contractor Misreads the Design

Plans get interpreted. Contractors make judgment calls. Without someone who knows the design inside and out available to answer in real time, those calls don't always go the right way. A coping material gets swapped. A pool wall ends up six inches off. A pergola beam is set too low. Small deviations become permanent features.

Late Changes Blow the Budget

Client indecision mid-build is one of the most common causes of cost overruns. Without a design expert available to help evaluate options quickly, decisions get delayed or made poorly — and change orders pile up. Meagen keeps choices grounded in the design so you move forward instead of backward.

The Finished Space Misses the Mark

The most expensive version of this problem is also the most heartbreaking — a finished backyard that doesn't feel like what you signed up for. The pool shape feels off. The paving pattern isn't quite right. The plants aren't what you imagined. Design liaison closes the gap between the vision and the reality.
